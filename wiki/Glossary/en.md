# Glossary
Common terms used in osu! and Rinari.

## A

**AR (Approach Rate)**
A difficulty setting that determines how long hit objects stay on screen before 
they need to be hit.

**Accuracy**
A percentage that represents how precisely you hit objects in a map.

## B

**BN (Beatmap Nominator)**
A community member who nominates beatmaps for ranking. See [[People/BN|Beatmap Nominators]].

**BPM (Beats Per Minute)**
The tempo of a song. Also affects slider speed and spinner difficulty.

## C

**Combo**
The number of objects hit in a row without missing. A higher combo means a higher score.

**CS (Circle Size)**
A difficulty setting that determines the size of hit circles and slider ends.

## D

**Drain Time**
The total length of a map excluding breaks.

## F

**FC (Full Combo)**
Completing a map without breaking combo.

## G

**GMT (Global Moderation Team)**
The team responsible for moderating the Rinari community. See [[People/GMT|GMT]].

## H

**HP (Health Points)**
A difficulty setting that determines how quickly your health drains and how much 
you recover from hitting objects.

**Hit Window**
The time frame in which a hit object can be hit and receive a certain score value 
(300, 100, 50).

## K

**Kiai**
A section of a map marked by flashing stars and increased visual effects, usually 
corresponding to the chorus of a song.

**Kudosu**
A form of recognition given to modders for helpful contributions to beatmap 
discussions.

## L

**Lead-in**
The time before the first hit object in a map.

## M

**Mod (Modification)**
A gameplay modifier that changes how a map is played, such as Hard Rock, 
Double Time, or Hidden.

**Modding**
The process of reviewing a beatmap and providing feedback to help improve it.

## N

**NAT (Nomination Assessment Team)**
The team that oversees Beatmap Nominators and manages the ranking process. 
See [[People/NAT|NAT]].

**NC (Nightcore)**
A mod that increases the BPM and pitch of a song.

## O

**OD (Overall Difficulty)**
A difficulty setting that determines the size of the hit windows for each 
score value (300, 100, 50).

**OS (Overmap/Overmapping)**
Placing hit objects that do not correspond to anything in the music.

## P

**PP (Performance Points)**
A metric used to measure a player's skill based on the difficulty of maps 
they have played and their accuracy.

## R

**Ranked**
A beatmap status indicating the map has passed through the nomination process 
and is eligible for pp and leaderboards.

## S

**SR (Star Rating)**
A numerical representation of a map's difficulty.

**SB (Storyboard)**
A visual element of a beatmap that plays alongside the music.

## T

**Taiko**
A drum-based game mode in osu!.

## U

**UC (Unstable Rate)**
A measure of timing consistency. A lower UR means more consistent timing.

## W

**WIP (Work In Progress)**
A beatmap that is still being worked on and is not ready for ranking.
