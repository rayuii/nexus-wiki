# Beatmapping
A guide to creating and submitting beatmaps on Rinari.

## Getting Started
To create a beatmap, you need the osu! editor. This is built into the osu! 
client and can be accessed by pressing `F4` or through the Edit button on 
the main menu.

## Creating a Beatmap

1. Open the osu! editor
2. Drag and drop your audio file into the editor
3. Set up your song metadata (title, artist, source, tags)
4. Set your timing points to match the song's BPM
5. Start placing hit objects

## Difficulty Spread
Your mapset should have a reasonable spread of difficulties so players of 
all skill levels can enjoy it. Common difficulty names include:

- Easy
- Normal
- Hard
- Insane
- Expert

## Submitting Your Map
Once your map is ready:

1. In the editor, go to **File** → **Upload Beatmap**
2. Your map will appear on Rinari's beatmap listing
3. Make sure your map follows the [[Guides/Ranking_Criteria|Ranking Criteria]]

## Getting Ranked
To get your map ranked on Rinari:

1. Make sure your map follows the [[Guides/Ranking_Criteria|Ranking Criteria]]
2. Ask for mods in the [Rinari Discord](https://discord.gg/fSmtpH3HSK) to 
   improve your map
3. Contact a [[People/BN|Beatmap Nominator]] to review your map
4. If the BN approves, they will nominate your map
5. A second nomination from another BN or [[People/NAT|NAT]] member will 
   qualify your map for ranking

## Importing from Bancho
If your map is already ranked on Bancho, you can request it to be imported 
directly. See [[Guides/Ranking_Criteria|Ranking Criteria]] for details.

## Tips
- Use the AiMod (`Ctrl+Shift+A`) to check for common issues
- Get feedback from other players before requesting a nomination
- Make sure your hitsounds are audible and appropriate
- Check your metadata carefully — artist name, title, and source should 
  be accurate
