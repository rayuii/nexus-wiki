# Ranking Criteria
Rules and guidelines that beatmaps must follow to be ranked on Rinari.
Based on the [osu! Ranking Criteria](https://osu.ppy.sh/wiki/en/Ranking_criteria).

- **Rules must not be broken under any circumstance.**
- **Guidelines may be ignored under exceptional circumstances.** A sufficient 
  explanation should be provided by the mapper when doing so.

## Importing from Bancho
Want a map that's already ranked on Bancho? Request it to be imported directly.

1. Go to the [Map Requests forum](https://rinarii.de/forum/11/create)
2. Make a post with the map you want imported
3. A staff member will review and import it if approved

## General

### Rules
- **No two hit objects can be placed on the same tick.** This includes hit circles 
  and the durations of sliders and spinners. osu!mania difficulties are exempt.
- **There must be at least 10 ms between a hit circle and the next object, and at 
  least 20 ms between the end of a slider and the next object.** osu!mania and 
  osu!catch difficulties are exempt.
- **There must not be any obscene imagery in the background, storyboard, or video 
  content.** This includes nudity, near-nudity, sexual references, violence, drug 
  abuse, etc.
- **There must not be any unused files or 0-byte files in the beatmap's folder.**
- **Difficulty settings must not use more precision than is possible in the beatmap 
  editor.**

### Guidelines
- **Kiai should start on a sound in the music.**

## Beatmap

### Rules
- **Every difficulty must have a minimum drain time of 30 seconds.**
- **All game modes within a beatmap must form a spread starting from the lowest 
  difficulty level dictated by the song's drain time.** The spread cannot skip any 
  difficulty levels and there cannot be any drastically large difficulty gaps between 
  any two difficulties.
- **Difficulty names must be clearly progressive and accurately indicate their 
  respective difficulties.**
- **A beatmap's custom difficulty naming must follow a common theme or pattern 
  related to the song or difficulty and must not be misrepresentative.**
- **Beatmaps cannot contain significant portions of difficulties belonging to other 
  users without their explicit involvement or permission.**

## Timing

### Rules
- **Beatmaps must be perfectly timed.** BPM and offset of each uninherited timing 
  point must be exactly synchronised with the song.
- **Uninherited timing points must be the same in every difficulty of a beatmap.**
- **No two uninherited or two inherited timing points can be placed at the same 
  point.**
- **Hit objects must be snapped within less than 2 ms of any timeline tick.**

## Audio

### Rules
- **The audio file must be encoded in MP3 or Ogg Vorbis.**
- **Average bit rate must not exceed 192 kbps for MP3, or 208 kbps for Ogg Vorbis.**
- **Average bit rate must not be lower than 128 kbps**, if such a source exists.
- **A beatmap may only contain one song file used by all difficulties.**
- **Beatmaps must be hitsounded.** Hitnormals give feedback to the player, and 
  additions accent the most important parts of the music.
- **Hitsounds must be audible.**
- **Preview points must be set and consistent between all difficulties of a beatmap.**
- **Every hitsound file must be at least 25ms long.**

## Video and Background

### Rules
- **You must have a background image on every difficulty of your beatmap.**
- **Background image requirements:**
  - Minimum width: 160px
  - Minimum height: 120px
  - Maximum width: 2560px
  - Maximum height: 1440px
  - Maximum file size: 2.5MB
- **A video's dimensions must not exceed 1280x720 pixels.**
- **A video must be encoded in H.264.**
- **A video's audio track must be removed from the video file.**

## Skinning

### Rules
- **If you are using any elements created by another community member, ask 
  permission beforehand.**
- **Gameplay elements must be visible.** You cannot make any element that will 
  impair the playability of the beatmap invisible.
- **Skinned elements must be cropped cleanly** so they do not have pixelated 
  artifacts or half-cropped shadows around them.
